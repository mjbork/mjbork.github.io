about me

My professional journey began at UIUC, serving as a videographer and editor while engaging in volunteer initiatives through the Graduate School of Library and Information Science (now iSchool) Community Informatics Corps. This involved collaborations on social entrepreneurship, community archives, and literacy programs. A brief period in Chicago was followed by relocation to Boston, expanding my multimedia expertise through involvement with PBS documentaries and similar projects, earning relevant certifications.

Since 2013, I have primarily lived in northwest Argentina, adapting to a new language and culture while delivering multilingual media campaigns in English, Spanish, and Portuguese. Leveraging advanced generative AI tools and traditional workflows, my deep interest in AI technology continually drives my creative strategies, recognizing AI’s integral role in digital content's future.

Additionally, my experience as an amateur boxer—including Illinois State Champion and Chicago Golden Gloves finalist—developed discipline, strategic thinking, and adaptability, traits I apply effectively to demanding professional environments.

Currently dividing my time between Argentina and the U.S., I seek professional collaborations that value strategic creativity, cultural sensitivity, and innovation.
